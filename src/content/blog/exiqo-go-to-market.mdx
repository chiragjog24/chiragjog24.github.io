Selling engineering services is hard. Selling *AI* engineering services is harder — because every buyer thinks they know what they need (a chatbot), and almost none of them know what they actually need (a capability transformation).

When we built the go-to-market strategy for EXIQO — R Systems' AI engineering practice — we had to solve three problems simultaneously: how to package AI engineering into something a sales team can pitch, how to price it so it's not just "bodies and hours," and how to deliver it in a way that creates measurable outcomes within 90 days.

## The Pod Model

We moved away from traditional staff augmentation toward productized pods. An AI Engineering Pod isn't a team of developers you rent — it's a pre-configured unit with defined inputs, outputs, and a methodology.

Each pod has a fixed composition (typically 4-6 engineers across different specializations), a fixed duration (90-day engagement), and defined deliverables at 30/60/90 day milestones. The client knows exactly what they're buying and exactly what they'll get.

## Pricing That Isn't Body-Shopping

The biggest shift was moving from hourly/monthly rates to outcome-based pricing. A pod engagement has a fixed price tied to deliverables, not headcount. If we can deliver the same outcome with AI-augmented engineers in less time, that efficiency accrues to us — which aligns our incentive to actually use AI effectively.

## The 90-Day Methodology

Every engagement follows a structured playbook. Month one is assessment and quick wins — baseline the client's current state, deploy AI tooling, and ship one visible improvement. Month two is depth — tackle the harder problems, build custom tooling if needed, and train the client's team. Month three is handoff and measurement — document everything, prove the ROI, and set up the client for self-sufficiency.

The methodology matters more than the technology. Clients don't buy AI — they buy confidence that their engineering will get measurably better.
